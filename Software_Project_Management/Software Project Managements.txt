Lecture 3:
    Understanding the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC):
        - Definition;
            -> Structured process used for developing information system
        - Importance in Software Development
            -> Provide a systematic approach
            -> Enhances Quality and manageability
            -> Clear communication with Stakeholders

        Models:
            = Waterfall Model
                Defination:
                    A linear and sequential approach
                Phases:
                    - Requirnments Gethering and Analysis
                    - System Designs
                    - Implimentation
                    - Integration and Testing
                    - Deployment
                    - Maintainance
                Advantages:
                    - Simple and Easy
                    - Well-documented, clear tracking
                    - well-dedfined requirnments
                Disadvantages:
                    - Inflexiable to change
                    - Late detection of defects can be costly
                    - Not ideal for complex or long-term projects
                Use Cases:
                    - Stable requirnments
                    - Industries like construction and manufacturing
            = Agile Model
                Definition:
                    An itrative and incremental approach
                Key Principles:
                    - Individuals and interactions over processes and tools
                        - better Communication leads to better performance
                    - Working softwares over comprehensive documentation
                    - Customer collaboration over contact negotiation
                        - value of ongoing collaburation with customers throughout the project
                    - Responding to change
                        - Flexibility of changes of phases, open to adjustments to better meet objectives.
            = Scrum
                Definition:
                    Focus on sprints
                Roles:
                        - Scrum master
                        - Product Owner
                        - Development Teams
                    Ceremonies:
                        - Sprint
                        - Sprint Planning
                        - Daily Standups
                        - Sprint Reviews
                        - Sprint Retrospective
                        - Product Backlogs
                        - Sprint Backlogs

                Advanteages:
                    - Highly Flexible and adaptable to changes
                    - Satisfactions, Flexiable for updates and Delivery
                Disadvantages:
                    - Required experenced team
                    - Budget and Timelines
                    - less emphasis on documentations
                Use Cases:
                    Evolving requirenemnts
                    Startups and innovative projects.

            = Itrative Model
                Definition:
                    Repeated cyles(Itrations) and in a smaller portions at a time
                Phases:
                    - Planning
                    - Design
                    - Implimentation
                    - Testing
                    - Evaluation
                Advantages:
                    - Incremental improvements
                    - Early Detection of issues
                    - Flexibily to incorporate changes in each itration
                Disadvantages:
                    - Increase in cost and time if itration not managed
                    - Required strong project management Knowledge
                Use Cases:
                    - Large and Comples projects
                    - Project where there is need for itrations.

        Best Model Selections:
            Factors:
                - Projects Requirnments
                - Project Size and Complexities
                - Costomer Involvement
                - Risk Management
                - Time and Budget Constraints
                - Team Expertises
            Decision-Making Framework
                - Assess requirenemnts stable:
                    - Stable -> Waterfall and Itrative
                    - Evolving -> Agile
                - Evaluating Project Size
                    - Large and Complex -> Itrative
                    - Small to Medium -> Agile or Waterfall
                - Consider Customer Involments
                    - High -> Agile
                    - Low -> Waterfall
                - Analyze Risk Factors:
                    - High risk requireing Flexibility -> Agile and Itrative
                    - Low risk with clear pathways -> Waterfall
                - Resource Availability
                    - Skilled teams For agile -> Agile
                    - Limited resources -> Waterfall or Itrative
