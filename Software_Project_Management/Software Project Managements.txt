    Understanding the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC):
        - Definition;
            -> Structured process used for developing information system
        - Importance in Software Development
            -> Provide a systematic approach
            -> Enhances Quality and manageability
            -> Clear communication with Stakeholders

        Models:
            = Waterfall Model
                Defination:
                    A linear and sequential approach
                Phases:
                    - Requirnments Gethering and Analysis
                    - System Designs
                    - Implimentation
                    - Integration and Testing
                    - Deployment
                    - Maintainance
                Advantages:
                    - Simple and Easy
                    - Well-documented, clear tracking
                    - well-dedfined requirnments
                Disadvantages:
                    - Inflexiable to change
                    - Late detection of defects can be costly
                    - Not ideal for complex or long-term projects
                Use Cases:
                    - Stable requirnments
                    - Industries like construction and manufacturing
            = Agile Model
                Definition:
                    An itrative and incremental approach
                Key Principles:
                    - Individuals and interactions over processes and tools
                        - better Communication leads to better performance
                    - Working softwares over comprehensive documentation
                    - Customer collaboration over contact negotiation
                        - value of ongoing collaburation with customers throughout the project
                    - Responding to change
                        - Flexibility of changes of phases, open to adjustments to better meet objectives.
            = Scrum
                Definition:
                    Focus on sprints
                Roles:
                        - Scrum master
                        - Product Owner
                        - Development Teams
                    Ceremonies:
                        - Sprint
                        - Sprint Planning
                        - Daily Standups
                        - Sprint Reviews
                        - Sprint Retrospective
                        - Product Backlogs
                        - Sprint Backlogs

                Advanteages:
                    - Highly Flexible and adaptable to changes
                    - Satisfactions, Flexiable for updates and Delivery
                Disadvantages:
                    - Required experenced team
                    - Budget and Timelines
                    - less emphasis on documentations
                Use Cases:
                    Evolving requirenemnts
                    Startups and innovative projects.

            = Itrative Model
                Definition:
                    Repeated cyles(Itrations) and in a smaller portions at a time
                Phases:
                    - Planning
                    - Design
                    - Implimentation
                    - Testing
                    - Evaluation
                Advantages:
                    - Incremental improvements
                    - Early Detection of issues
                    - Flexibily to incorporate changes in each itration
                Disadvantages:
                    - Increase in cost and time if itration not managed
                    - Required strong project management Knowledge
                Use Cases:
                    - Large and Comples projects
                    - Project where there is need for itrations.

        Best Model Selections:
            Factors:
                - Projects Requirnments
                - Project Size and Complexities
                - Costomer Involvement
                - Risk Management
                - Time and Budget Constraints
                - Team Expertises
            Decision-Making Framework
                - Assess requirenemnts stable:
                    - Stable -> Waterfall and Itrative
                    - Evolving -> Agile
                - Evaluating Project Size
                    - Large and Complex -> Itrative
                    - Small to Medium -> Agile or Waterfall
                - Consider Customer Involments
                    - High -> Agile
                    - Low -> Waterfall
                - Analyze Risk Factors:
                    - High risk requireing Flexibility -> Agile and Itrative
                    - Low risk with clear pathways -> Waterfall
                - Resource Availability
                    - Skilled teams For agile -> Agile
                    - Limited resources -> Waterfall or Itrative

TOPIC: Understanding the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC)

Def - structure process used for developing information security and software efficiently 
Importance:
1. Provide systematic approach
2. Enhance quality and management of software
3. facing clear communication among stakeholder

SDLC Models
- Waterfall Model: A linear and sequential where each phase must be complete before the next begin.
Phases:
Requirement Gathering and Analysis, System Design, Implementation(Coding), Integration and testing, Deployment, Maintenance.
Adantage:
Simple and easy, well documented, suitable of project with well defined requirements
Disadvantage:
Inflexible, Late detection of defects, Not ideal for complex 
Use Case:
suitable of project with well defined requirement 

Agile Principles:
Individual and interaction over processes and tools
Working software over comprehensive Documentation
Customer Collaboration over Contract Negotiation
Responding to change over following a plan

Framework 
SCRUM - focus on sprints 
Sprints: A time boxed iteration 1-4 week where a usable increment are created
Roles: Scrum Master, Product Owner, Development team
Ceremonies: Sprint planning(Define the goals and work for upcoming sprint), daily standups(a 15 min daily meetings),
sprint reviews(Held at the end of sprint between stakeholders), sprint retrospective(No stakeholders involved between development team)
Artifacts: Product backlog(An ordered list of everything needed of whole project), sprint backlog(Ordered list of everything but for a specific sprint), increment 
Advantages:
Highly flexible and adaptive to change.
Disadvantage:
can be challenging to predict timelines and budget
Communication because if big Teams

Iterative Model:
Develops the system through repeated cycle(Iteration) and in smaller portion at a time 
Phases: Planning, Design, Implementation, Testing, Evaluate 
Advantages:
Allows for incremental improvement
Early detection of issue
flexibility changes in each iteration 
Disadvantages:
- increased cost and time
- require strong project management 
Use case:
Large and complex project 

Choosing the right SDLC Model:
Factors to Consider:
- project requirement
- project size and company
- customer involvement 
- Risk management
- Time and budget 
- Team Expertise 

Developing Project Charter - A document that formally authorize a project or project phase
Components:
- Project purpose or justification
- Measurable objectives 
- High level requirements
- Assumptions and Constraints 
- High level Risk
