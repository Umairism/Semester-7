## Vowels & Special Characters (Umlauts)

German has special vowel characters called **Umlauts** and the **Eszett (ß)**:

| Character | Name | Pronunciation |
|:-:|:-:|:-:|
| Ä / ä | Umlaut A | Sounds like **"Ae"** |
| Ö / ö | Umlaut O | Sounds like **"Oeu"** |
| Ü / ü | Umlaut U | Sounds like **"Ew"** |
| ß | Eszett (Beta / sharp S) | Sounds like **"ss"** |

> **Note:** Umlauts modify the sound of the base vowel, making it fronted or rounded. The Eszett (ß) is a special ligature representing a double "s" sound.

### Personal Pronouns & Verb Conjugation

German verbs change their endings based on the subject pronoun. Here are two common verbs — **kommen** (to come) and **wohnen** (to live):

| Pronoun | English | kommen | wohnen |
|:--|:--|:--|:--|
| **ich** | I | komme | wohne |
| **du** | you (informal, singular) | kommst | wohnst |
| **er / sie / es** | he / she / it | kommt | wohnt |
| **wir** | we | kommen | wohnen |
| **ihr** | you (plural) | kommt | wohnt |
| **Sie** | you (formal) | kommen | wohnen |
| **sie** | they | kommen | wohnen |

> **Tip:** The formal "Sie" (you) and "sie" (they) share the same verb conjugation. Context and capitalization distinguish them.

### 21–40

German numbers 21–99 are formed by saying the **ones digit first**, then **"und"** (and), then the **tens digit**:

> Example: 21 = *ein **und** zwanzig* (one and twenty)

| # | German | # | German |
|:-:|:--|:-:|:--|
| 21 | einundzwanzig | 31 | einunddreißig |
| 22 | zweiundzwanzig | 32 | zweiunddreißig |
| 23 | dreiundzwanzig | 33 | dreiunddreißig |
| 24 | vierundzwanzig | 34 | vierunddreißig |
| 25 | fünfundzwanzig | 35 | fünfunddreißig |
| 26 | sechsundzwanzig | 36 | sechsunddreißig |
| 27 | siebenundzwanzig | 37 | siebenunddreißig |
| 28 | achtundzwanzig | 38 | achtunddreißig |
| 29 | neunundzwanzig | 39 | neununddreißig |
| 30 | dreißig | 40 | vierzig |

> **Note:** The number 30 (*dreißig*) uses **ß** instead of a "z" like other tens (zwanzig, vierzig, etc.).
